On the long silence — what we learn from sittings without speech.

Mira leads our Sunday morning sittings, and once a quarter she holds a four-hour silent vipassana for whoever wants to come. The signup is small. The room fills anyway. After the last one, I asked her why she keeps offering them when so few people register. She said: "The room knows when to fill itself."
