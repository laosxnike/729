1 · Stripe Checkout

    How to connect Stripe:
    1. Log in to dashboard.stripe.com.
    2. Go to Developers → API keys. Copy your Secret key (starts with sk_live_ or sk_test_).
    3. Add it as STRIPE_SECRET_KEY in Vercel (see button below).
    4. For each product/event you sell, go to Products in Stripe, create it, and copy the Price ID (starts with price_).
    5. Paste those Price IDs into your Google Sheet's priceId column, or put them in PRODUCT_PRICE_MAP as JSON.

    2 · Email Notifications recommended

      How to connect email (Resend — free tier):
      1. Create a free account at resend.com.
      2. Go to API Keys and create a key. Copy it.
      3. Add it as RESEND_API_KEY in Vercel.
      4. Add your email address as OWNER_EMAIL in Vercel.
      Once set, all form submissions (reservations, contact, rentals, practitioner bookings) will send you an email.

      3 · Google Sheets (Live Data)

        How to connect Google Sheets:
        1. Create a new Google Sheet with the columns shown below.
        2. Go to File → Share → Publish to web.
        3. Choose the correct tab, select Comma-separated values (.csv), and click Publish.
        4. Copy the URL and paste it into Vercel as PRODUCTS_CSV_URL, EVENTS_CSV_URL, or PRACTITIONERS_CSV_URL.
        5. To show an item, set its status column to active. Leave it blank or set it to draft to hide it.

        4 · Webhooks (optional, for Zapier / Make)

          If you set up Zapier or Make automations, paste the webhook URL into the corresponding env var below. Webhooks take priority over email notifications when both are set.
          • RESERVATION_WEBHOOK_URL — free event RSVPs
          • CONTACT_WEBHOOK_URL — contact & rental forms
          • PRACTITIONER_INTAKE_WEBHOOK_URL — practitioner applications
          • PRACTITIONER_BOOKING_WEBHOOK_URL — practitioner session requests

          5 · Practitioner Intake Link

          Send this link to any practitioner you want to invite. They fill in their name, bio, discipline, pricing, and photo link. You receive their submission by email (or webhook), then add them to your Practitioners sheet to publish.

          6 · Add Environment Variables

          All settings are managed in Vercel. Click the button below to open your project's environment variable settings (you must be logged in to Vercel).

          Open Vercel Environment Settings →

          After adding or changing env vars, click Redeploy in Vercel for changes to take effect.
